I am writing to apply for the R\&D Process Engineer position in TSMC's
Corporate Research division. My Ph.D.\ in Applied Chemistry, grounded in
Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Physics, has given me a strong foundation
in semiconductor materials, surface and thin-film chemistry, and hands-on
process characterisation---areas I am eager to focus on ALD and ALE research
as part of TSMC's next-generation logic device development.

My most directly relevant experience is my research on two-dimensional
materials. I have studied transition metal dichalcogenide (TMD) materials
extensively, combining an off-axis microscope with transient grating
photoluminescence at sub-80\,fs temporal resolution to probe carrier dynamics
and optical properties in these atomically thin semiconductors. This work
gave me deep familiarity with the physics of 2D material systems, their
surface sensitivity, and the characterisation techniques needed to understand
their electronic and optical behaviour---a background that pairs naturally with
ALD-based channel and gate-dielectric engineering in advanced logic devices.
The ability to connect material-level properties to device-relevant performance
metrics is, I believe, precisely the kind of insight TSMC's corporate research
team is looking for.

Beyond 2D materials, my research background spans a broad range of thin-film
and surface chemistry contexts that are foundational to ALD and ALE process
development. I have investigated surface passivation chemistry and
charge-transfer heterogeneity in polycrystalline perovskite thin films,
co-localising transient absorption microscopy with SEM to connect nanoscale
surface and interface phenomena to device-level performance. I have grown and
characterised II--VI semiconductor crystals by chemical vapour transport,
applying the Chernov bulk diffusion model to simulate and optimise growth
conditions from first principles. These experiences reflect exactly the kind
of fundamental-principles-driven problem-solving and hands-on materials
exploration that this role requires. I am certified in SEM and am comfortable
with XRD, steady-state and time-resolved photoluminescence, and UV--Vis
spectroscopy as part of a broad characterisation toolkit.

I take strong personal ownership of lab work and have a consistent record of
delivering complex experimental projects independently across multiple
postdoctoral research environments in Taiwan. At the same time, I am an
effective collaborator: my work has required close coordination with research
groups at universities, external equipment vendors, and interdisciplinary
teams spanning chemistry, physics, biology, and engineering---precisely the
kind of university, research centre, and supplier engagement described in this
role. I document findings rigorously and communicate results clearly in both
written reports and oral presentations to specialist and non-specialist
audiences alike.

I am already based in Taiwan and fully committed to working at TSMC
headquarters. As a permanent resident under the Plum Blossom Talent Program
(APRC holder), I hold an open work permit; no residency sponsorship or working
permit support is required. I would be delighted to bring my 2D materials
expertise, thin-film process knowledge, and hands-on research approach to
your corporate research team.

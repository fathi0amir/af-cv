I am writing to apply for the Software Service Engineer position at Nearfield
Instruments in Hsinchu. My Ph.D.\ in Applied Chemistry, grounded in
Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Physics, is complemented by extensive
hands-on experience in Python-based software development, high-precision
instrument operation, and direct technical collaboration with
multidisciplinary teams. I am excited by the opportunity to support
leading chipmakers with NFI's next-generation AFM metrology platform---
combining my software skills with a genuine understanding of the underlying
measurement science.

Programming and software engineering are genuine strengths I bring to this
role. I have extensive experience writing Python code for automation,
signal processing, image analysis, data pipeline development, and algorithm
debugging across both Windows and Linux environments. I regularly use version
control (Git), structure code for maintainability, and build tooling that
others can use and extend. I have also leveraged LLM-based AI coding tools
heavily as part of my development practice---rapidly prototyping solutions,
generating and reviewing diagnostic scripts, and accelerating analysis
workflows. Beyond scripting, I am comfortable reading system logs and
telemetry to trace root causes, working across software--hardware interfaces,
and diagnosing performance issues related to throughput, latency, and
resource utilisation---all core to the troubleshooting responsibilities of
this role.

System-level thinking and hands-on instrumentation underpin my research
career. I have designed, built, and maintained complex, multi-subsystem
measurement platforms---femtosecond pump--probe microscopes, off-axis
interferometric assemblies, and single-particle tracking setups---each
requiring end-to-end ownership from installation and qualification through
performance validation and fault diagnosis under operational pressure. I am
accustomed to writing and following qualification procedures, restoring
systems to specification independently, and translating observed failure
modes into structured, actionable reports for engineering teams---directly
mirroring the installation, bring-up, and feedback-loop responsibilities
described in the job posting.

On the semiconductor and metrology side, I am certified in scanning electron
microscopy and have co-localised transient absorption microscopy with SEM
to investigate nanoscale structural heterogeneity in semiconductor thin films.
I have worked with advanced 2D materials and photovoltaic device structures
throughout my career, giving me solid grounding in the process control
challenges that advanced fab metrology must address. This background lets
me engage credibly with customer engineering teams on the measurement
problems NFI's AFM platform is solving, not just on the software layer
above it.

I thrive in fast-paced, ownership-driven environments with real customer
impact. My postdoctoral career has required regular direct engagement with
external partners and equipment vendors---presenting findings, negotiating
specifications, and converting field observations into engineering
requirements. I adapt readily when priorities shift and am comfortable
operating in ambiguous, high-stakes situations. I am fluent in both Mandarin
and English, my daily working languages throughout my career in Taiwan, and
I am fully prepared for cleanroom fab presence, domestic travel, and the
Rotterdam training rotations the role requires.

I am already based in Hsinchu and available to begin immediately. As a
permanent resident of Taiwan under the Plum Blossom Talent Program (APRC
holder), I hold an open work permit with no sponsorship requirement.
I would be delighted to bring my software engineering skills, instrumentation
depth, and semiconductor metrology background to NFI's field team.

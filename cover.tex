I am writing to apply for the Systems Engineer position at KLA. My Ph.D.\ in
Applied Chemistry, grounded in Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Physics, has
given me over a decade of hands-on experience designing, building, validating,
and supporting complex optical and opto-mechanical measurement systems---a
background I believe maps directly onto KLA's need for a systems-level technical
owner capable of driving customer escalations, supporting fielded metrology
tools, and contributing to the development of next-generation inspection and
metrology platforms.

Optical systems engineering and performance characterisation are at the core
of my research career. I have conceived and built several advanced measurement
platforms entirely from scratch: femtosecond pump--probe laser scanning
microscopes, off-axis interferometric assemblies, transient grating
photoluminescence systems achieving sub-80\,fs temporal resolution, and
lock-in amplifier based signal-acquisition chains. Each project required
end-to-end ownership---from architecture definition and opto-mechanical design
through component selection, alignment, performance validation, and
troubleshooting---giving me the system-level depth that KLA's escalation and
sustainment work demands. My sub-diffraction imaging work and phase-sensitive
detection experience provide a strong physical foundation for understanding
the interferometric, reflectometric, and defectivity measurement principles
underlying KLA's product portfolio.

I have a demonstrated record of resolving technically ambiguous problems under
pressure. Across multiple research institutions I have independently diagnosed
and repaired complex instrument failures---often restoring systems to operation
without external support and against project deadlines. I have co-localised
transient absorption microscopy with SEM to map nanoscale charge-transfer
heterogeneity in semiconductor films, directly connecting physical root causes
to measurable system-level signatures---an analytical discipline that translates
naturally to root-cause identification and corrective-action implementation in
customer escalation scenarios. I am certified in SEM operation and comfortable
with a broad range of characterisation modalities, which enables rapid
contextualisation of field data against known failure modes.

On the data and software side, I have extensive working knowledge of Python and
MATLAB, applied across signal processing, statistical analysis, image
processing, and automation of measurement pipelines. I am accustomed to
designing experiments and interpreting data within rigorous physical models
rather than relying on empirical heuristics---a practice that accelerates
convergence on root cause and produces corrective actions that hold.
I regularly document findings in formal technical reports, define measurement
specifications, and present results clearly to audiences ranging from domain
specialists to engineering managers.

Throughout my postdoctoral career in Taiwan I have coordinated closely with
multidisciplinary teams spanning chemistry, physics, biology, and engineering,
as well as with external equipment vendors, translating customer and project
requirements into concrete technical expectations and delivering solutions on
schedule. I am comfortable taking ownership of technically ambiguous problems,
operating autonomously in fast-paced environments, and engaging constructively
with customers onsite. I am fluent in English and functional in Mandarin, and
I am fully prepared for the international travel this role requires.

As a permanent resident of Taiwan under the Plum Blossom Talent Program
(APRC holder), I hold an open work permit; no residency sponsorship or working
permit support is required from KLA. I would welcome the opportunity to bring
my optical systems depth, semiconductor characterisation experience, and
escalation-ready problem-solving approach to your team.

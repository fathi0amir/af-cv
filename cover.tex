I am writing to apply for the R\&D Nano Patterning Technology Development
(NPTD) Engineer position at TSMC. My Ph.D.\ in Applied Chemistry, grounded in
a Bachelor's and Master's in Physics, has given me deep expertise in optics,
photonics, ultrafast lasers, and optical simulation---areas I regard as directly
relevant to advancing high-NA EUV lithography and optical patterning at the N2,
A14, and A10 nodes. I am genuinely passionate about contributing to world-leading
semiconductor technology and welcome the opportunity to apply rigorous,
first-principles science to the manufacturing challenges at the frontier of the
industry.

My most directly applicable expertise lies in precision optical system design
and laser-based instrumentation. Over more than a decade of research, I
designed and built several advanced optical platforms entirely from scratch:
femtosecond pump--probe laser scanning microscopes, off-axis interferometric
setups, and transient grating photoluminescence systems achieving sub-80\,fs
temporal resolution. This depth of experience in optical alignment, aberration
management, coherent light--matter interaction, and signal optimization
transfers directly to the optical simulation and instrumentation demands of EUV
and advanced lithography development. My sub-diffraction imaging work---resolving
features below the classical optical limit---has furnished me with physical
intuition for the contrast and resolution physics that govern nano patterning.
I am also certified in scanning electron microscopy (SEM) operation, a core
metrology tool for patterning verification and failure analysis.

On the materials, instrumentation, and data side, my research spans the growth
and characterization of semiconductor and photovoltaic materials---including
XRD Rietveld refinement, steady-state and time-resolved photoluminescence, and
UV--Vis spectroscopy---alongside custom electronic circuit design, simulation,
and fabrication for low-noise signal acquisition, and full-stack programming of
multi-instrument DAQ systems incorporating PMT, APD, EMCCD, and iCCD detectors.
I have extensive working knowledge of Python, MATLAB, C\#, and C++, applied
across simulation pipelines, image processing, and data analysis. My analytical
workflow integrates statistical and model-driven experimental design consistent
with SPC and DOE frameworks, and I actively incorporate machine learning methods
for pattern recognition and data-driven process modelling---capabilities that
align directly with NPTD's requirements for predictive patterning simulation,
new measurement technology development, and process yield optimization.

I am experienced in cross-functional collaboration, having worked closely with
physicists, chemists, biologists, engineers, and equipment vendors across
multiple research institutions in Taiwan. I take strong personal ownership of
instrumentation and process deliverables, adapt readily to shifting priorities,
and am committed to hands-on participation from early prototype through to
production-ready implementation. As a permanent resident of Taiwan under the
Plum Blossom Talent Program (APRC holder), I hold an open work permit;
no residency sponsorship or working permit support is required from TSMC. I
would be delighted to contribute this combination of optical, materials, and
computational expertise to your nano patterning development team.

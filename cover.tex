I am writing to apply for the R\&D Nano Patterning Technology Development
(NPTD) Engineer position at TSMC. My Ph.D.\ in Applied Chemistry, grounded
in Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Physics, has given me over a decade of
hands-on experience in optical physics, advanced semiconductor metrology,
process-level problem-solving, and AI-assisted data analysis---a background
I believe maps directly onto the lithography process development, optical
simulation, measurement technology, and yield-improvement responsibilities
of this role.

Optical physics and quantitative measurement are at the core of my research
career. I have designed and built femtosecond pump--probe laser scanning
microscopes, off-axis interferometric assemblies, and transient grating
systems, each requiring rigorous understanding of coherence, diffraction,
phase contrast, and light--matter interaction---the same physical foundations
that underlie advanced lithography, EUV exposure, and patterning simulation.
I have applied optical simulations and physical modelling extensively to
predict and interpret material and process behaviour, moving beyond empirical
heuristics to model-driven explanations. This first-principles approach to
analysis directly matches TSMC's requirement for technical problem-solving
grounded in fundamental rather than empirical models.

Process characterisation and failure analysis on semiconductor and thin-film
materials have been a consistent focus throughout my career. I am certified
in scanning electron microscopy (SEM) and have co-localised transient
absorption microscopy with SEM to map nanoscale process heterogeneity,
connecting root causes at the materials level to measurable process-level
signatures. I have systematically applied Design of Experiments (DOE)
principles to structure measurement campaigns and Statistical Process Control
(SPC) thinking to monitor and interpret process stability---both of which are
central to yield improvement and early-stage mass-production assessments in
an NPTD context.

Programming and AI are genuine strengths I bring to this role. I have
extensive experience writing Python and MATLAB code for signal processing,
image analysis, experiment automation, and statistical modelling. Beyond
scripting, I have hands-on experience leveraging large language model (LLM)
based AI coding tools to accelerate research workflows---rapidly prototyping
algorithms, automating data pipelines, and building analytical tools that
would otherwise require significantly more development time. I am comfortable
applying machine-learning methods for pattern recognition and anomaly
detection in process and metrology data, and I actively incorporate AI
assistance as a core part of my technical practice.

Throughout my postdoctoral career in Taiwan I have collaborated with
multidisciplinary teams spanning physics, chemistry, and engineering, worked
alongside external equipment vendors, and adapted readily as project scopes
and priorities evolved. I am fluent in English and proficient in Mandarin,
both of which I use daily in Taiwan, and I am comfortable with the intensive
fab presence and hands-on process ownership this role demands.

As a permanent resident of Taiwan under the Plum Blossom Talent Program
(APRC holder), I hold an open work permit with no sponsorship requirement.
I would welcome the opportunity to contribute my optical physics depth,
process characterisation experience, and AI-augmented programming capability
to TSMC's nano patterning technology development.

I am writing to apply for the Reliability Engineer position at TSMC. My
Ph.D.\ in Applied Chemistry, grounded in Bachelor's and Master's degrees in
Physics, has equipped me with the fundamental analytical and experimental
skills---spanning semiconductor materials, thin-film characterisation, electronic
circuit design, and quantitative data analysis---that I believe are directly
relevant to reliability testing, hotspot pattern investigation, and process
change risk assessment in an advanced IC fabrication environment.

Robust, model-driven data analysis is central to my research practice. Across
more than a decade of experimental work, I have designed and executed systematic
measurement campaigns on photovoltaic and semiconductor devices, interpreted
time-resolved spectroscopic and imaging data using first-principles physical
models, and used statistical methods to distinguish genuine process signals from
noise. This analytical discipline maps directly to reliability data testing,
monitoring, and the kind of rigorous hotspot and WAT methodology development
that TSMC's reliability group requires. I am experienced in tracking measurement
trends over time and flagging anomalies before they propagate into yield or
product risk---habits built through years of operating sensitive instruments
where process drift must be caught early.

On the materials and process side, I have studied degradation and charge-transfer
behaviour in polycrystalline perovskite photovoltaic films by co-localising
transient absorption microscopy with SEM, directly connecting nanoscale
structural features to device-level performance failure---an approach analogous
to root-cause reliability analysis in IC fabrication. My familiarity with
semiconductor processing chemistry, thin-film deposition physics, and surface
passivation strategies gives me a solid foundation for evaluating how fab
process changes translate into circuit and product reliability risk. I am also
certified in SEM operation, comfortable with XRD and photoluminescence
characterisation, and experienced in building custom analogue and digital
electronics for precise signal acquisition, which supports hands-on
participation in test methodology development.

I am an effective communicator in both English and Mandarin (daily working
language throughout my time in Taiwan), and I have a consistent record of
presenting complex technical findings clearly to multidisciplinary audiences
and writing detailed technical reports. I have collaborated across research
groups spanning chemistry, physics, biology, and engineering, and I take strong
personal ownership of my project deliverables---qualities that align with
TSMC's expectations for reliability engineers who also interface directly with
customers on reliability concerns.

As a permanent resident of Taiwan under the Plum Blossom Talent Program
(APRC holder), I hold an open work permit; no residency sponsorship or working
permit support is required from TSMC. I would be glad to contribute my
analytical rigour, semiconductor materials knowledge, and hands-on measurement
experience to your reliability engineering team.
